Welcome to My Page!

Hello! I'm Francesco Dettori, a passionate **PhD student in Biomedical Engineering** at **INRIA**. My research focuses on the exciting intersection of technology and medicine, particularly in the fields of **simulations, digital twins, and advanced medical technologies**.

I believe that cutting-edge engineering can revolutionize healthcare, leading to more personalized treatments, enhanced diagnostic capabilities, and a deeper understanding of human physiology. My work involves developing computational models and virtual representations of biological systems to explore complex phenomena and predict outcomes.

My Research Interests

My primary research interests include, but are not limited to:

  • Biomedical Simulations: Developing and utilizing sophisticated computational models to simulate biological processes and medical interventions.
  • Digital Twins in Healthcare: Creating virtual replicas of human organs, patients, or even entire populations to monitor, analyze, and predict health outcomes.
  • Medical Technology Development: Contributing to the design and improvement of innovative medical devices and diagnostic tools.
  • Computational Physiology: Applying computational methods to understand the functions of the human body in health and disease.
  • Image-Guided Interventions: Exploring how medical imaging can be integrated with simulations for more precise surgical planning and execution.

My goal is to contribute to a future where engineering solutions empower clinicians and improve patient lives.
